Common Questions

Can I really make roses that look professional? +
Absolutely! Our step-by-step tutorial is designed for complete beginners. With the right template and our guidance, your roses will look like they came from a florist.
Do I need a Cricut machine? +
Not necessarily! While Cricut makes it easier and more precise, you can cut the templates by hand with good scissors. We include PDF printable versions for hand cutting.
How long does it take to make one rose? +
Once you get the hang of it, about 15-20 minutes per rose. Your first one might take 30-45 minutes as you learn the technique.
What's the best paper to use? +
65lb cardstock works perfectly. It's thick enough to hold shape but flexible enough to curl. Avoid regular printer paper (too flimsy) or cardboard (too thick).
Can I sell roses I make with these templates? +
Yes! The templates come with a commercial license, so you can sell your finished paper roses at craft fairs, on Etsy, or anywhere else.
